Responsive Redesign Homework

0. Introduction

This webpage is to showcase my work for the Responsive Redesign assignment for CS1300.
In this assignment, I chose an existing website to analyse and redesign to improve the website's usuability and accessibility across different types of viewing screens.

1. Choosing a Website

For this assignment, I chose the website for Jaswell's Farm in nearby Smithfield, RI.

Link to the website here

(Disclaimer: one part of the Jaswell's Farm website to make apple picking reservations stopped working while I was working on this assignment. Below the 2 pictures of the tractor and the blueberries, there was functionality to select the number of people and the date of the reservation, both of which I included in my redesign. I'll lightly reference to this now-nonexistent part of the website.)


The reason I chose this website was because it is not responsive at all, and the information on the website seems clunky and confusing.

Problems with Exsiting Website

Usability

  • The information after the "Welcome to Jaswell's Farm" banner may not be that important to users (e.g. where the farm owners went to college). This may distract and divert any users trying to make a reservation on the main page.
  • Navigation bar covered by photos in the middle of the page, another nav bar appears at the bottom of the page

Learnability

  • Navigation bar at top of screen helps new users easily go to other pages of the website
  • Have to scroll all the way to bottom of the page to make a reservation and finding hours of operation is in a big block of text, may be hard to find for new users.
  • Images in middle of website are actually photo galleries with multiple images, arrows to show different images only appear if you hover over the images

Memorability

  • The hierarchy may be confusing for casual users. They may be confused at first if they can make a reservation on the website since it's at the bottom. These users may expect clearer icons or some big buttons/links to make the reservation.

The current website also does not adjust to different screens well. For example, the nav bar doesn't turn into any sort of hamburger menu and the images do not scale with the viewport size.

5. Takeaways

This was a rewarding assignment, I found myself considering a wide range of design choices when redesigning the Jaswell's Farm website. I now definitely take well-designed websites for granted because I ran into issues with even the smallest of changes.

I'd consider my redesign a good improvement of the existing website: I fixed the text-heavy current Jaswell's Farm site with new information and navigation buttons. There are more improvements I could make, especially since I don't know the needs and wishes Jaswell's Farm has for their website. Moving forward, I would send my current redesign and iterate it based on given feedback.
