Prototyping a Roommate-Matching App

Iteratively Designing a Product's Interface

01. Picking a Startup

In this demo we will be designing a product's interface from scratch for a company whose product we have not seen. The criteria for our selection is as follows:


  • The company must be in an accelerator class of 2022.
  • The company's product must be centered around a digital interface.
  • The company must have a clear purpose and view.

Keeping the above critera in mind we decided to design an interface for the company Roomkast!
As stated in their profile on Techstars.com:
Roomkast is a profile-matching tool for people who see the value in co-sharing a place to live.

02. Sketching & Wireframing

To begin, each member of our team created a set of low fidelity wireframes detailing what we imagined the interface to look like, without any input from our peers.

Person 1

The first member of our group incorporated apartment preference into the roommate search. Users would first find an apartment that they liked first and would then reach out to potential housements interested in the same apartment.

Person 2

The second member of our group focused on developing a platform that would algorithmically determine compatibility between users after you create your profile. This platform was only created to match users based on their living style preferences and had no consideration for potential apartments.

Person 3

The third member of our group created a survey that users were required to fill out before they could interact with other on the platform. This survey algorithmically determined compatibility and once completed, allowed users to see people they had a high chance of living well with.

Person 4

The fourth member of our group considered the ability to create both individual and group profiles. This solution addresses how, for example, if there are three people who know they want to live together and are considering a four-bedroom apartment, they can enter Roomkast as a group seeking one more roommate.

04. User Testing

Although we were happy with the mockup we had at the time, we sought to improve our design even more by incorporating user testing! User testing is when you have someone use the interface/product that you have created and give feedback and suggestions about your design. To accomplish this we used the platform UserTesting.

For each of our users, we gave them the following task:
Imagine you’re a young professional looking to share an apartment with a roommate to cut down on rent. Create an account and find a potential roommate you would like to chat with.

Specific steps to complete the task:

  1. Select a login method and create an individual profile.
  2. Follow the prompts to create an account.
  3. Next, look through the potential housemate candidates – you can interact with the discovery page and look at their profiles.
  4. Before you check your messages, reach out to Amelia (a potential housemate) by sending her a message.
  5. After you send your message to Amelia, pin her conversation in the messages tab.
  6. Lastly, sort the conversations by compatibility.

Let's view the results!

Note: You can view the original feedback submissions by clicking the links at the top of each summary.

User 1:

  • User 1 had trouble sending the message to Amelia since the text and button disappeared. Additionally, he had trouble pinning Amelia’s conversation in the “Messages” tab since sliding to reveal the pin and the pressing to pin the conversation was a bit unintuitive. Lastly, this user had an issue with sorting by compatibility as he found the button to be very small and not noticeable.

User 1's written response to questions

  • Would you like to see more, less, or the same information on the cards on the discovery screen? Which elements would you like to see added or removed?
    • "Absolutely the same - I’d remove elements on messages screen which show preview message"
  • What did the bookmark shaped icon below each profile indicate to you?
    • "That it would highlight what my preferences were and those that I want to short list. It would be good if that person was also notified that I’d bookmarked them"
  • What do you think about the design of the app?
    • "Very clean, fresh and easy to follow. I wasn’t overwhelmed with colours and I was able to very swiftly navigate through the screens. Really liked it. Nice blues as well"

User 2:

  • User 2 also had issues with sending Amelia a message due to the same reason as User 1; additionally, he found the pinning task to be unintuitive as well. User 2 also did not fully understand the transition from the survey wireframes to the “Discovery” page as he was unsure if he had completed the survey.

User 2's written response to questions

  • Would you like to see more, less, or the same information on the cards on the discovery screen? Which elements would you like to see added or removed?
    • "It’s the right amount of information"
  • What did the bookmark shaped icon below each profile indicate to you?
    • "The ability to save the profile to be able to visit later"
  • What do you think about the design of the app?
    • "Clean, easy to use, not very visually compelling but it’s good enough for its purposes"

User 3:

  • Although User 3 did not actually complete the task according to our metrics, she did rate the task as completed in her reflection. The message sending, pinning, and sorting tasks were unintuitive, as she was unable to complete these actions.

User 3's written response to questions

  • Would you like to see more, less, or the same information on the cards on the discovery screen? Which elements would you like to see added or removed?
    • "Everything just needs to be a lot more clearer"
  • What did the bookmark shaped icon below each profile indicate to you?
    • "Bookmark could mean to save a particular profile"
  • What do you think about the design of the app?
    • "Very simple to the point and also bright I loved colors and the fonts chosen "

The main issues regarding user experience were 1) the message to Amelia did not appear correctly, leading to some confusion and 2) the pinning interaction was not responsive and ultimately confusing. Users also experienced issues with needing to scroll on their phones to see the entire screen (leading to issues seeing buttons if they were hidden at the bottom of a page). Generally, users enjoyed the cleanliness of the design coupled with the fonts and colors chosen. Additionally, all three users believed that they were able to complete the task successfully and rated the easiness of the task as a ⅘.

05. Final Analysis and Takeaways

In response to the feedback we received from our user testing, we fixed the issue with messaging Amelia, this time ensuring that the message and send button were both visible on the wireframe. With regards to pinning, we determined that because users were using iPhones during these tests, the interaction on Figma did not go through correctly – when we tested the interaction on our desktops, we were able to complete the task successfully. Thus, we request users to use a desktop when interacting with the prototype. Additionally, we made the “sort by” button on the “Messages” tab larger so that users could find it more easily

In future iterations, we will adjust the scrolling issue to make each page more responsive to varying screen sizes. Additionally, we will flush out other interactions such as archiving and deleting messages.
